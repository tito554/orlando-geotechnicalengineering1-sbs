Electrical Resistivity Surveys and VES in Orlando, FL

Together, we solve the challenges of tomorrow.

DISCOVER →

A common misstep on Orlando construction sites is assuming subsurface uniformity across a lot simply because the ground surface looks consistent. When foundation designs proceed without verifying electrical resistivity profiles, hidden variations in sand, clay lenses, or karst features can remain undetected. In Orange County, where the Hawthorne Group sediments and surficial sands create abrupt lateral changes, vertical electrical sounding (VES) resolves layer resistivities before a single footing is poured. The technique maps water table depth, clay content, and potential dissolution zones that affect bearing capacity and corrosion risk. Combining resistivity data with a CPT survey sharpens the interpretation where cone refusal is expected, while MASW profiling adds shear-wave velocity for seismic site class determination per ASCE 7-22.

In Orlando's karst terrain, a resistivity anomaly above 500 ohm-m often indicates a dry void or air-filled cavity that requires geotechnical follow-up.

Method and coverage

ASTM D6431-18 sets the field procedure for dipole-dipole and Schlumberger arrays used across central Florida. In Orlando, where the water table often sits within 1.5 to 3 meters of the surface, resistivity contrasts between dry sand, saturated sand, and clay are pronounced. The team uses a multi-electrode system with 56 to 84 electrodes, switching automatically through programmed sequences to build a 2D resistivity section below each transect. For deeper investigations exceeding 30 meters, vertical electrical sounding with expanding Schlumberger spreads is preferred. Data inversion runs with RES2DINV or EarthImager, applying solid constraints to suppress artifacts. When karst voids are suspected, the resistivity survey is paired with seismic refraction to cross-validate low-velocity zones against high-resistivity anomalies, reducing false positives in sinkhole-prone areas.

Regional considerations

The field crew deploys a stainless-steel electrode array powered by a DC resistivity meter with a 200-watt transmitter, injecting current while measuring potential difference across receiver dipoles. In Orlando's built-up areas, buried utilities, reinforced concrete slabs, and metal fences create cultural noise that distorts apparent resistivity readings. The geophysicist on site identifies coupling problems early and adjusts array geometry or electrode grounding to maintain data quality. Saltwater intrusion along the Floridan aquifer interface is another local factor, producing resistivity lows below 10 ohm-m that must be distinguished from natural clay layers. The interpretation report flags zones where apparent resistivity suggests fill material, cavities, or aggressive soil conditions, referencing FDOT Standard Specifications for Earthwork when resistivity governs corrosion potential for buried structures.

Top questions

How much does an electrical resistivity survey cost in Orlando?

For typical Orlando-area projects, electrical resistivity surveys range from US$630 to US$1,160 depending on line length, target depth, and site access conditions. A fixed-price proposal is issued after reviewing the site location and survey objectives.

How deep can a VES sounding go in central Florida?

Vertical electrical sounding with a Schlumberger array can reach depths of 60 to 80 meters when electrode spreads are extended to 200 meters or more. The actual penetration depends on near-surface resistivity and the transmitter power available.

Does electrical resistivity work in urban environments with underground utilities?

Yes, but the geophysicist must account for metallic pipes, conduits, and reinforced concrete, which introduce conductive or resistive anomalies. Electrode lines are offset from known utilities, and data filtering removes coupling artifacts during inversion.

What resistivity values indicate a sinkhole or cavity in Orlando?

Dry air-filled cavities typically produce resistivity anomalies above 500 ohm-m, while clay-filled depressions show values below 20 ohm-m. Interpretation combines resistivity with local geology, water table depth, and, when available, seismic velocity data.
